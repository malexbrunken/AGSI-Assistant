# AGSI Policy Document

## Overview

This document contains policies that govern AGSI operations. Policies are added as we encounter issues or make decisions that need to be documented.

---

## Policy 001: Certification Exam - Not Pay-to-Play

**Effective Date:** March 2026

### Purpose
Ensure compliance with ANAB/ISO 17024 requirements and maintain certification integrity.

### Policy Statement
Customers are purchasing the **opportunity to take the certification exam**, not the certification itself. This is not "pay-to-play" — certification is earned through passing the exam, not purchased.

### Rationale
- ANAB requires certification to be exam-based, not course-based
- Cannot require training to certify
- Must ensure exam has psychometric validity
- Candidates must demonstrate competence to earn certification

### Implementation
- All product descriptions state "Exam Access and Certification Attempt"
- Welcome emails clarify this is an opportunity, not a guarantee
- Branding kit only provided after passing exam

## Policy 002: Exam Retake Policy

**Effective Date:** March 2026

### Purpose
Ensure candidates take exam preparation seriously while maintaining accessibility.

### Policy Statement

| Attempt | Waiting Period | Fee |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| First attempt | N/A | Included in original purchase |
| Second attempt (first retake) | 7 days minimum | FREE |
| Third attempt+ | 7 days minimum | $39 per attempt |

### Requirements

1. **7-Day Waiting Period**
   - Candidate must wait at least 7 days after a failed attempt before retaking
   - Purpose: ensures adequate study time

2. **Post-Fail Communication**
   - Email notification of failure
   - Link to test preparation materials
   - Encouragement to study before retaking

3. **Data Integrity**
   - Each attempt is tracked in database
   - Statistics reported to ANAB must reflect genuine competency

### Rationale

Without a retake policy:
- Candidates might "click through" to see questions without preparing
- Randomized questions make this ineffective anyway
- But it would skew pass-rate statistics presented to ANAB

**Target Pass Rate:** ~75% on first attempt

A 75% pass rate demonstrates:
- Exam is accessible to qualified candidates
- Questions are appropriately challenging
- Credential has value (not everyone passes)

A pass rate significantly lower (e.g., 50%) could indicate:
- Exam is too difficult
- Candidates aren't prepared
- Questions aren't valid indicators of competence

### Exceptions
- Contact CEO for exceptional circumstances
